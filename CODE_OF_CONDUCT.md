# Code Of Conduct

Airo should be a useful place for developers to learn, contribute, and disagree
constructively.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and direct.
- Keep discussion technical, specific, and actionable.
- Assume contributors have different experience levels, devices, time zones, and
  language backgrounds.
- Critique code, docs, designs, and tradeoffs without attacking people.
- Credit community reports, reproductions, fixes, and design feedback.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, slurs, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Doxing or sharing private information without consent.
- Spam, astroturfing, fake engagement, or manipulative promotion.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or PRs after maintainers ask to refocus.
- Publishing secrets, private logs, or sensitive user data.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; lock threads; close issues or
PRs; or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior harms the
project or community.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers through the contact path in
[`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) if the concern involves sensitive information. For
ordinary moderation problems, use a GitHub issue or discussion and include links,
screenshots, and context.

Reports should include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Any relevant links or timestamps.
- Whether there is immediate risk to a person, account, or system.

Maintainers should acknowledge sensitive reports as soon as practical and avoid
retaliation against good-faith reporters.
